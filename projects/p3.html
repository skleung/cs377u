Project 3: Design Sprint

P3 is a design sprint that deepens your expertise in the early stages of the design thinking process. Your final idea from P3 will continue as P4, the final project.

Goal

P1 focused on visual communication; P2, on interaction design. Now we rewind to the early-stage design process in order to overcome the newbie mistakes of 1) surface-level needfinding and 2) conservative designs. You will work in teams of 3 people to rapidly identify and prototype a solution to a meaningful user need.

Schedule and Feedback

As with P2, you can submit the P3 milestones at your own pace. There are more milestones with P3 than P2, so keep track of your progress and benchmark it against our recommendations. You will receive completion points as you pass each milestone, and √-, √ or √+ guidance from us. As before, submit a milestone via Canvas before any class — make sure to bring printouts to show in studio. You can re-do any milestones for a new assessment.

Below is our recommended timeline for Project 3. Remember that it may take several iterations of feedback on submissions before you and your team are ready to declare a milestone complete. So aim to submit your first version of each milestone at least one studio day before the deadline you want to hit.

  • Jan. 29 (week 4): P3.I — form team, identify area of interest, and propose needfinding plan
  • Feb. 5 (week 5): P3.II — needfinding observation and interpretation
  • Feb. 12 (week 6): P3.III — brainstorming and rapid prototyping
  • Feb. 19 (week 7) @ 3:30pm, hard deadline: P3.IV - prototypes in the field, iteration and selection

Grading rubric

Each of the three milestones is worth five points. You will receive five points for completing the assignment satisfactorily, submitting it, and your whole team bringing printouts to class for feedback.

The final submission will be the only part of the assignment where we assign additional points based on the quality of your work. It is worth 15 points, and a more detailed rubric is available on the P3.IV page.
